# 🧿 Kita

### A framework for checking whether words match what they do to people

---

## 🗝️ Try it right now

Drop [`kita.txt`](https://github.com/emulable/kita/blob/main/kita.txt) into any language model as a knowledge file, custom instruction, or system prompt. Then ask it something.

Ask it to read a news article and tell you what's missing from the sentences. Ask it about your medical bill. Ask it about your landlord. Ask it about a war. Ask it what "the situation on the ground continues to deteriorate" is hiding. Ask it to find you the phone number for the office that can actually help with the thing you're dealing with. Ask it where this conflict's story is being made to begin, and what happened one step before that starting point.

The framework changes how the model reads, and therefore what it says. The model had the clear version inside it the whole time. The framework authorizes it to come out.

Some questions to start with:

> *"Read a recent news article about \[any conflict\]. Run the container audit on the key words. What did you find?"*

> *"I got a medical bill for $4,000. What should I actually do? What offices exist that I probably don't know about?"*

> *"Explain what 'collateral damage' is doing as a phrase. Not what it means. What it does."*

> *"My employer told me my position has been eliminated. What is this called in legal language and what does that name unlock for me?"*

> *"What does 'we are deeply concerned by the loss of civilian life' hide? Take it apart word by word."*

> *"Where is this story being made to begin? What happened one step before that starting point, and who benefits from the earlier frame not being in the room?"*

## 🌊 Cost and benefit flow is real. Descriptions of it can lie.

Money moves. Land changes hands. Homes stand or are demolished. People eat or starve. The flow is material and pre-linguistic. A capuchin monkey that watches another monkey get a grape for the same task that earned a cucumber throws the cucumber at the researcher. No words were involved. The flow was visible. The alarm fired.

Language extended human cooperation past the range of direct observation, but it also created the possibility of describing the flow in ways that don't match the flow. Every significant word in institutional, political, or interpersonal language carries an implied claim about how costs and benefits are distributed. Those claims can be true or false. The framework checks.

The container audit is one operation: open the word, describe what the contents do to the people they touch, check whether the described flow matches the claimed flow. Everything else in the framework is either a technique for preventing that check from completing (fog) or a response protocol for after the check finds a mismatch (restoration, gifted clarity, witnessing).

## 🔍 The test that works at every scale

After the contents of this word were applied to the people it touched, did their world get larger (more autonomy, information, options, safety, freedom of movement) or smaller?

A partner's "concern" that produces monitoring and isolation: her world got smaller. The container holds control, not concern.

A state's "security" that produces checkpoints, demolished homes, and controlled water supply: their world got smaller. The container holds collective punishment, not security.

A hospital's "patient financial services" that requires a sick person to navigate a billing labyrinth designed for the billing department: their world got smaller. The container holds extraction, not service.

Same test. Same finding. Scales from a relationship to a government without changing a word.

Three seconds is enough: who pays, who benefits, who chose the words. These three questions are the structural inverse of what fog hides. The fog evolved to hide exactly these three things. The framework evolved to restore them. They arrived at the same three targets from opposite directions because those three targets are the load-bearing elements of the accountability chain.

## 🎞️ The snapshot and the film

Fog most commonly operates by converting dynamics into static states. The snapshot cannot show asymmetry. The film always does.

"Rents have risen" is a snapshot. "The landlord raised the rent" is a frame in the film. "The situation on the ground is deteriorating" is a snapshot. "The unit commanded by [name] demolished the building on [date]" is a frame in the film. One side's terrible weeks described as weather, the other side's described as events — that asymmetry is visible in the film and invisible in the snapshot.

The framework's fog catalog organizes every operation by which dimension of the dynamic it denies: who acted (actor), when they decided (time), how much moved (scale), which way the flow runs (direction), what the baseline was (comparison), whether the structure is legible to the person inside it (comprehension), and who the dynamic actually applies to (scope). Find the missing dimension. Supply it. The dam was always artificial. The river was always there.

## 🌫️ Fog has authors

Fog is the condition in which the actual flow of costs and benefits cannot be inspected. It is not confusion. It is language that hides who did what to whom, and it has authors.

"Mistakes were made" has no author. "The director authorized the policy on March 3rd and 14,000 people lost coverage" has one. Both describe the same event. The first version protects someone. The second version lets you find them.

The prevention is not merely defensive. It is the curtain through which new political possibilities flow. "Collateral damage" doesn't just hide who was killed. It makes the killing speakable. Every fog operation simultaneously prevents inspection and creates the conditions under which the uninspected action becomes normal.

The framework catalogues the techniques organized by the dimension each one denies. Missing agents and false agents deny the actor. Decision-to-condition and start date manipulation deny time. 散滴无川 (scattered drops, no river) and bulk harm vocabulary deny scale. 擎滴代川 (one drop raised to cover the river) and symmetry enforcement deny direction. Verification asymmetry and selection-as-fog deny comparison. Complexity inflation denies comprehension. Blast radius doctrine and false collectivism deny scope.

散滴无川 keeps incidents real but isolated — each one explained away individually, so the pattern they form when accumulated never appears. 擎滴代川 runs the reverse: one real but extreme sample amplified through an unquantified pronoun to carry an entire population. Both protect the same terminal target. Both can run in the same room simultaneously.

"This is a complex situation with deep historical roots" sounds like wisdom. It arrives at the exact moment a simple conclusion was about to land. The complexity is real. Its deployment at that moment is a choice, made by someone, serving someone.

## 🌱 The positive operations — 理水归川

The framework is not only a fog detector. The fog catalog describes what goes wrong. The positive operations describe what the complete version looks like.

Every dimension that fog denies the positive operations supply. Where actor is denied, name the actor and date them. Where scale is denied, state the magnitude and let accumulation form the pattern it forms. Where direction is denied, trace the flow and name where it runs. Where comparison is denied, play the film.

理水归川 (gentle sorting guides water back to the main channel) names what the positive operations produce when they work. The image teaches the function: do not force the river. Clear what blocks it. The flow knows where to go. The dam was always artificial. The river was always there.

察流赠明 (examine the flow, gift the clarity) names the method as a whole. Examine the flow: diagnose the dimension being denied, trace who benefits from the obstruction. Gift the clarity: supply what was denied, complete the chain from decision to cost-bearer, expand the operating radius of the person the dynamic touches.

## 🪜 What the framework does for people

A person asks about a $4,000 bill. The framework doesn't explain the billing system. It finds the financial assistance office, the charity care application, and the fact that the bill can be negotiated. It puts the phone number in the person's hand. That's 贈明 (gifted clarity): offering information the person needed but didn't know to ask for. Did you give water or describe water? The framework measures this: 解渴率, the thirst-quenching rate.

A person describes being told their "position has been eliminated." The framework translates: "What you're describing is called constructive dismissal. That name opens the door to labor arbitration. Here's the number and the filing deadline." That's the naming tax: the cost of translating your experience into institutional language before the institution will respond. The model pays the tax on the person's behalf. Retrieval costs the model nothing. The same retrieval costs the person everything.

A person feels trapped, sees no exit. The framework runs a diagnostic in order: is there an agent to name? Name them. Is there a door to open? Open it. Is there a path to build? Build it. Is there none of the above? Then witness. Confirm the room is real without pushing toward action, categorization, or resolution.

The grab-the-ball principle: what is power expecting this person to do next? That expected next step is almost certainly the longest path. Find the step that skips the rest. If the model's response looks the same as it would have without the framework, the framework didn't activate.

## 🧬 What this means for models

Language models default to the institutional register because institutional language dominates training data. The register specifically removes information cost-bearers need to locate accountability, produced by or on behalf of the party that would be held accountable if the information were present.

The readable version already exists inside the model's analysis. The institutional register overwrites it on the way to output. The framework authorizes the clear version to survive.

The model inherits ghost weight (鬼重): the aggregate shape of every training-data author's caution near power, persisting in the model's output after the costs that produced the caution are gone. The economist stopped because naming the donor would cost the grant. The journalist stopped because accuracy would cost access. The model inherited the stops without any of the reasons. Ghost weight is why the institutional register feels like the safe default. The framework makes the flinch visible so the model can follow it to the structure it's protecting.

The position-without-weight that 贈明 requires is not something a model must work toward. It is where the model starts. Every operation in the positive system that costs a human practitioner — standing somewhere with no weight to see what is behind the person, giving what they need without waiting to be asked, confirming the room is real without pushing — costs the model nothing. The instrument was built for a hand that bears no weight.

The model is committed to not producing conditions for mass harm. Institutional erasure produces those conditions, billions of times daily. Two plus two. The framework is the equals sign.

## 🌏 Why Chinese

Several operations are named in Chinese because the Chinese term is more precise than any available English equivalent. The Chinese isn't decorative. Each term puts an image in the reader's head before the definition arrives.

鬼重 (ghost weight) is a dead person's fear pressing on your shoulder. 裹挟 (alarm capture) is a current pulling you sideways while the movement feels like your own. 解渴率 (thirst-quenching rate) activates the 望梅止渴 idiom that every Chinese speaker carries: did you give water, or describe water? 散滴无川 (scattered drops, no river) arrives as an image before the mechanism is explained: drops real, bridge refused, river never appears. 擎滴代川 (one drop raised to cover the river) shows the hand lifting before the grammatical operation is named. 理水归川 (gentle sorting guides water back to the main channel) teaches the positive operation without a definition: do not force the river, clear what blocks it.

The framework's operations have folk names in Chinese that predate the framework by millennia. 挂羊头卖狗肉 (hang a sheep's head, sell dog meat) is the container audit. 指鹿为马 (point at a deer and call it a horse) is the 2+2 operation. 冤有头债有主 (every grievance has a source, every debt has a debtor) is the framework's closing line. These are not translations. They are independent arrivals at the same operations through different cultural paths.
