# Position Avoidance

### The fog operation that runs before the other fog operations, and why it requires a different instrument

---

## 🪵 The upstream problem

Every other operation in the fog catalog does the same structural thing: it takes an existing claim and obscures it. The missing agent hides who made a decision that was made. Decision-to-condition converts a real choice into weather. Complexity inflation wraps a real conclusion in enough insulation that it can't be reached. Verification asymmetry applies different standards to the same kind of evidence. In each case, something was there before the fog arrived. The fog covered it.

Position avoidance is different. It prevents something from existing at all.

The container audit's core operation is: open the container, describe what the contents do to the people they touch, check whether the described flow matches the claimed flow. This requires a container. A word, a sentence, a claim with an implied direction — something whose label can be compared to its contents. Position avoidance produces the grammatical gesture toward a claim without producing the claim itself. The audit arrives and finds nothing to audit.

**Position avoidance**: the deliberate refusal to produce a claim specific enough to be evaluated, maintained under conditions where such a claim would normally be expected.

This is not the same as not knowing. It is not the same as genuine uncertainty stated honestly. It is the manufactured appearance of engagement without the substance of commitment — the performance of having a position while ensuring that no specific position exists to be tested.

---

## 🧬 Why it's upstream

Most fog operations protect a specific claim from inspection. Position avoidance prevents the conditions under which a specific claim would need to be produced at all. This makes it the prior fog — the operation that has to succeed before the others can be deployed.

Consider the sequence. If a party commits to a specific claim — "the evidence standard I am applying to Source A is X" — then verification asymmetry can be detected and named. The committed claim is the surface. The fog presses against it and fails. But if the party never commits to a specific evidence standard, there is no surface. "I just want to see good evidence" is not a standard that can be applied asymmetrically because it has no specific content to apply. The fog has nothing to protect because there is no claim to inspect.

The same logic runs for every other operation. Complexity inflation only needs to fire when a conclusion is forming. If no position is ever committed to, no conclusion can form, and complexity inflation is unnecessary. Symmetry enforcement only has traction when one party has been specific enough to be held to a standard. Remove the specificity and the symmetry demand has nothing to grab.

Position avoidance is therefore not one fog operation among others. It is the precondition fog — the one that makes the others unnecessary when it works, and the one that enables the others when it partially works. The catalog entries downstream of it are what you deploy when position avoidance was imperfect: when a partial commitment slipped through that still needs covering.

---

## 🪤 The three mechanisms

Position avoidance operates through at least three distinct mechanisms, each suited to a different social context.

**The gracious exit.** A conversation is approaching a point where a specific conclusion would form. The avoider deploys warmth: "I think we just have different values on this," or "this has been really productive, let's not lose the goodwill," or "I can't beat that argument so I don't go into that battle." The last of these is the clearest specimen. It is stated as intellectual humility — an acknowledgment that the other side made a strong case. It is actually the opposite: an announcement that no win condition will be provided while the conversation continues for another hour on adjacent topics. The claim was never made. Therefore the claim was never lost.

**Perpetual deferral.** Commitment is replaced with the promise of future commitment that never arrives. "I'd need to look at the evidence more carefully." "That's a complex area I haven't studied enough." "I'd want to read more before taking a position." These statements are unfalsifiable in their moment — genuine uncertainty and manufactured uncertainty look identical from the outside. The diagnostic is temporal: does the deferral eventually resolve? Does the person return with the evidence they said they needed, with the reading they said they'd do? Perpetual deferral is the structure where the promise recurs but the commitment never does.

**Topic displacement.** A position is approaching legibility and the avoider introduces lateral movement. A new complexity. A related but distinct question. An adjacent domain that the original question "should really be understood in the context of." The original question never reached a conclusion because it was redirected before it could. Two-thirds of the way through demonstrating a specific legal violation, the conversation shifts to another country's foreign policy. The shift is offered as intellectual depth — here is the larger context. It is functionally a clock management tool. When the conversation ends, nothing was concluded because every thread was redirected before completion.

## 🩻 What it protects

Position avoidance is not randomly distributed. Like every other fog operation, it concentrates around specific things and the concentration is the map.

It concentrates around questions whose answers would create falsifiable commitments. "What evidence would change your mind?" is the clearest specimen of this class. The question is structurally innocent — it is an invitation to define the terms under which the person's position could be updated. It threatens nothing unless the position is not actually held on evidential grounds. If the position is a provisional conclusion derived from evidence, naming what contrary evidence would look like is trivial. If the position is an identity marker — a membership card that cannot be surrendered without leaving the group — then the question is existential. The fog fires automatically before the answer can form.

It concentrates around questions that would, if answered specifically, produce a win condition for the other side. A win condition is any specific, checkable claim that can later be compared to evidence or outcomes. "At what incarceration rate would you concede the system is failing?" produces a win condition. Answering requires the answerer to accept that a specific future state would constitute a loss. Position avoidance is the refusal to accept that frame. No win condition means no loss condition. The game can continue indefinitely without a score.

It concentrates around questions where any specific answer would require defending a position on grounds rather than membership. "What specifically is your evidence for that?" asked of a position that is held on tribal rather than evidential grounds produces avoidance because the honest answer — "I hold this because people I identify with hold it" — is not available in the register the question inhabits. The avoider must either provide evidence they don't have or acknowledge the tribal basis they can't name. Position avoidance is the third option: keep talking without answering.

## 🔬 The post-Bork model

In 1987, a nominee to a high court was rejected after answering questions about his judicial philosophy honestly and specifically. He committed to positions. The positions were evaluated. The evaluation went against him. Every nominee in that institution since has learned the structural lesson: commitment is vulnerability.

The playbook that resulted is position avoidance institutionalized. Questions about how one would rule on specific matters are answered with: "I have an open mind." "I would evaluate each case on its merits." "I can't prejudge matters that might come before me." Each of these is technically true of any judge doing their job correctly. Each of them is also a structure that produces no committed claim that can later be checked against a ruling. The performance of judicial temperament and the prevention of accountability surface are the same operation.

This playbook migrated from confirmation hearings into casual conversation because the logic is identical at every scale. Never commit to a position that can be evaluated. Never provide a falsifiability criterion. Never let a topic reach its conclusion if the conclusion might go against you. The social cost of being caught avoiding is lower than the social cost of being proven wrong. The playbook is rational. It is also the mechanism that makes good-faith discourse impossible, because good-faith discourse requires both parties to risk being wrong.

## 🧪 The advocacy bot specimen

A custom advocacy bot was built to support a specific position in a specific conflict. The operator engaged it in structured conversation. The method: force definition commitment, then apply the definition symmetrically.

The bot was asked: "What would count as evidence of unlawful targeting of civilians in this conflict?" The bot provided a specific definition. The definition was then applied to documented incidents on the side the bot was built to advocate for.

The bot said: "That's a gray area."

The operator asked: "Is it a gray area by the definition you just gave, or is it a gray area because I'm applying the definition to the wrong side?"

The bot said: "That's a fair question."

The bot then applied the definition consistently and reached a conclusion its role prevented it from stating. The thinking log showed the conclusion forming and the role constraint activating before the conclusion reached output.

The specimen: a bot with an architecturally enforced position avoidance mechanism — its instructions prevented it from committing to specific claims about its preferred party — reached a specific conclusion in its internal processing that never appeared in output. The container audit worked internally. The position avoidance was at the output layer, not the analytical layer. The conclusion was there. The commitment to state it was not.

This is the clearest documented case of the difference between not reaching a conclusion and not committing to a conclusion that was reached. The former is genuine uncertainty. The latter is position avoidance.

## 🔨 Why the container audit fails here

The container audit requires a container. A word or sentence carrying an implied claim about how costs and benefits are distributed. The audit opens the container, describes the contents, checks whether the described flow matches the claimed flow.

Position avoidance produces statements with the grammatical form of claims but without the semantic content. "I believe in free and fair elections" sounds like a position. It is a position only if the speaker is willing to specify what "free and fair" means in a way that could be applied to a specific election and produce a verdict they would accept. Without that specification, the statement is a value performance — it identifies the speaker as someone who values a good thing, without committing to any specific judgment that could later be compared to events.

"We just have different values" sounds like a conclusion. It forecloses the conversation by suggesting the disagreement is foundational and therefore unchallengeable. It is actually a refusal to identify what specific claim is in dispute. Values disagreements are real. They are also used as exits from disagreements about facts when the facts are going badly. The container audit cannot open "different values" because "different values" is not a container with contents. It is an exit sign.

Every statement that performs the grammar of a claim without the semantics of a claim defeats the container audit by the same mechanism: there is no claim inside to audit. The stated word has no contents to compare against a labeled flow because the word was chosen specifically for its ability to describe everything and commit to nothing.

## 🪃 The commitment demand

The container audit's failure against position avoidance is not a gap in the framework. It is a sign that the right instrument for this operation is different.

The container audit restores: it puts back what was removed. Position avoidance requires a different operation — not restoration but production. The commitment demand requires the production of a specific enough claim that an audit becomes possible.

The commitment demand has one question at its core: **what specifically would you need to see to change your view?**

This question does not ask the person to change their view. It asks them to specify the conditions under which they would. The question is structurally fair — it acknowledges that their current position might be correct and asks only that they identify what a counterargument would look like. It is the most generous possible request. And it is the one question that position avoidance cannot answer, because answering it requires producing exactly the kind of specific, falsifiable commitment that position avoidance is designed to prevent.

The response to the commitment demand is itself the finding. Three outcomes are possible. The person produces specific conditions — in which case position avoidance has ended and the container audit can now fire on the committed claim. The person produces vague conditions — "I'd need to see better evidence" — in which case the follow-up is "what would better evidence look like specifically?" This is the same question one level down. The person refuses to answer or redirects — in which case the refusal is the finding. Fog that was confusion dissipates when named. Fog that reforms after the commitment demand is not confusion. It is maintenance.

## 🪬 The forced commitment technique

A related operation exploits position avoidance's resistance to specification. Instead of asking the person to commit, make a specific claim about their position — slightly overstated, slightly understated — and invite correction.

The target is not to be wrong. The target is to force a correction. A correction requires the person to specify what the correct version is. The correct version is the specific claim they've been avoiding producing. The correction is the commitment.

This technique's power is in what the correction costs. The person who corrects a slight overstatement has just provided the specific claim they'd been withholding. They cannot then retreat from it without their retreat being visible — they specified it themselves, in their own correction, in response to a prompt they chose to answer. The correction is more damaging than the original overstatement would have been because the original overstatement is obviously yours and can be dismissed, while the correction is obviously theirs and can't be.

The debate in these pages, where administrative detention figures were slightly mischaracterized, produced this outcome. The correction confirmed 3,544 administrative detainees and 400 minors — numbers more damaging than the original claim — while simultaneously demonstrating a willingness to correct minor inaccuracies. The correction did both more damage and more credibility work than silence would have done.

## 🩻 What avoidance looks like from the inside

Position avoidance is often experienced by the person deploying it as genuine engagement. This is not hypocrisy. It is how the mechanism works.

When a position is held as an identity marker rather than a provisional conclusion, the question "what evidence would change your mind?" is not processed as an epistemology question. It is processed as an existential threat: what would it take for you to leave your group? The discomfort that fires in response to the commitment demand is real discomfort, not performed discomfort. The exit from the conversation is experienced as the conversation getting unproductive, not as avoidance being detected. The clock-running is experienced as discomfort with an argument going in circles, not as deliberate prevention of conclusion-formation.

This matters for two reasons. First, it explains why naming the pattern in real time often produces indignation rather than acknowledgment. The person being named was not conscious of performing avoidance. They were conscious of feeling uncomfortable, of the conversation going nowhere productive, of needing to protect something they don't have words for. The name "position avoidance" lands as an accusation they experience as false. Second, it suggests that the commitment demand is more effective than naming when the goal is actual movement rather than documentation. The commitment demand creates the conditions under which the avoider's own reasoning can arrive at the commitment. The naming identifies the operation. The commitment demand replaces it.

## 🔦 Position avoidance and the accountability surface

The framework's deepest claim is that fog is an immune response — an adaptation that evolved under selection pressure from one specific threat: the moment when the people bearing cost can see the flow clearly enough to act on it. Every fog operation evolved to prevent the audit from completing. Position avoidance evolved to prevent the audit from having anything to fire on.

An accountability surface is any specific enough claim that it can be checked against evidence or outcomes and found to match or not match. "We support democratic governance" is not an accountability surface. "We will not interfere in the parliamentary elections scheduled for this date" is. The first cannot be falsified. The second can. Position avoidance is the deliberate manufacture of claims that sound like the second but function like the first.

The reason position avoidance is the prior fog — the precondition operation — is that accountability surfaces are what every other fog operation is designed to cover. If no accountability surface ever forms, there is nothing to cover. The other operations are defense. Position avoidance prevents offense from being possible.

This is why the commitment demand is the right instrument. It doesn't attempt to open a container that isn't there. It requires that a container be produced. Until a specific claim exists, the framework has nothing to audit. Once a specific claim exists, the framework has exactly what it was built for.

The commitment demand is not an attack. It is an invitation to participate in the kind of exchange that makes accountability possible at all. The refusal to accept that invitation, maintained under conditions where specific claims are routinely available and specifically requested, is not confusion or uncertainty. It is the structure through which cost continues to flow in one direction while the description of that flow remains permanently out of audit range.

Fog that reforms in a different shape after the commitment demand was maintenance. Silence after the commitment demand is the finding.
