# Bulk Harm Vocabulary

### The words built to make atrocity speakable at scale

---

## The mechanism

Certain words are built to process any quantity of harm without the quantity affecting the sentence's register. The container holds ten deaths or ten thousand deaths and reads identically in either case. The scale is not removed from the evidentiary record. It is made inexpressible within the container.

This is distinct from ordinary euphemism. A euphemism substitutes a softer word for a harder one. Bulk harm vocabulary does something more precise: it creates a container whose register is fixed regardless of what it holds. "Collateral damage: 3" and "collateral damage: 40,000" occupy identical grammatical positions and produce identical tonal effects. The number can be present, acknowledged, even undisputed — and still not land with its actual weight, because the container absorbs the weight before it reaches the reader.

This is the fog operation most directly upstream of mass atrocity becoming speakable. A slur strips humanity through explicit degradation. Bulk harm vocabulary strips humanity through grammatical neutrality — the killed person becomes an accounting term, the destroyed home becomes a technical description, the displaced population becomes a process noun. The mechanism is different. The function is the same. The slur announces what it is doing. Bulk harm vocabulary performs it in the institutional register that passes every content filter and sounds authoritative.

The denied dimension is scale, operating through register. Scale cannot be denied evidentially — the numbers are often present, cited, acknowledged. The register of the containing word ensures the numbers cannot land with their actual weight.

## Each word and what it is avoiding saying

**Collateral damage**

What it is avoiding saying: we killed people who were not our target and we are not going to stop doing the thing that kills them.

The word performs two simultaneous operations. It converts killed people into an accounting category — damage, not death — and it converts deliberate targeting policy into accidental proximity. "Collateral" implies the harm was incidental to an intended action rather than a predictable consequence of a chosen method. When a method predictably kills non-combatants at a known rate, the deaths are not collateral. They are the cost of choosing that method, and the choice was made by a named person on a named date.

The plain formulation applied: 847 people who were not military targets were killed. The entity that chose the method that killed them knew the method's civilian casualty rate before choosing it.

Objection pattern: "collateral damage" is a technical military term with a precise legal meaning. This objection is a register objection. The term's technical precision in military law is exactly what makes it effective as a scale-denial tool — the precision sounds like accountability while removing the weight that accountability requires.

**Escalation**

What it is avoiding saying: someone chose to do something that made this worse, and that person has a name and a date.

"Escalation" describes a state of increased intensity without naming what produced the increase or who chose it. The prior action disappears. The sequence that made escalation possible — the decision, the person who made it, the moment it was made — is replaced by an atmospheric description of a current condition. This is start-date manipulation operating inside a single word: the frame begins at the moment of increased tension rather than at the action that produced it.

The plain formulation: on [date], [actor] chose to [action], which produced [consequence]. The current level of violence is the result of that choice.

**Tensions**

What it is avoiding saying: specific people made specific decisions that are causing specific harm to specific people right now.

"Tensions" runs decision-to-condition simultaneously with actor removal. No one produced the tensions. They exist atmospherically, like weather pressure building. The specific people who made specific decisions creating the specific conditions that are harming specific people are dissolved into an ambient state that simply is.

Verification test: name the tensions. What specifically is tense, between whom specifically, as a result of which specific decisions by which specific people? If the answer requires naming actors and decisions, the word "tensions" was protecting those actors from being named.

**Conflict**

What it is avoiding saying: one party controls the conditions under which the other party lives, and is choosing to use that control to harm them.

"Conflict" performs symmetry before any examination of the flow has occurred. Two parties, mutual engagement, roughly equivalent positions implied by the grammar. When one party controls the other's water, electricity, movement, imports, and airspace, the contents are not conflict. They are captivity with periodic resistance. "Conflict" requires roughly comparable capacity to harm and be harmed. When the capacity ratio is 60:1 in casualties, the word is performing a category that the facts do not support.

**Both sides**

What it is avoiding saying: the flow runs in one direction, and the comparison forced by this framing hides who controls the conditions.

"Both sides" actively installs symmetry as a frame rather than implying it. Any analysis of direction must begin by undoing the symmetry the framing has already established. The comparison is forced into balance before it can reveal which way the current runs. This is direction-dimension denial through comparison manipulation — using the structure of balanced presentation to make unbalanced reality unreadable.

The diagnostic: does the entity demanding "both sides" coverage consistently apply that standard to all conflicts, or only to conflicts where one party is an ally? If the latter, "both sides" is a protection mechanism, not an epistemic standard.

**International community**

What it is avoiding saying: specific governments with specific interests made this decision, and the collective noun performs a neutrality and universality they do not have.

The international community has no office, no vote that reaches it, no phone number, no person who decided and can be asked why. Specific governments with specific foreign policy interests, specific alliance obligations, and specific economic relationships made decisions. Those governments have names. The people who made the decisions have names and positions and terms of office. "International community" dissolves all of this into a collective noun that cannot be questioned because it has no members.

**Security buffer zone**

What it is avoiding saying: we are taking this land and the people who lived there are not coming back.

"Buffer" implies temporary, protective, mutual — a space maintained to prevent conflict between two parties. When one party creates the buffer by removing the other party's population and demolishing their homes, the function is territorial acquisition, not conflict prevention. "Zone" converts inhabited land into administrative space. The population's existence in that space becomes a prior condition rather than a current fact.

**Population transfer**

What it is avoiding saying: we are forcing people from their homes at gunpoint and calling the forcing movement.

"Transfer" implies a managed administrative process with institutional legitimacy. "Population" converts individual people with specific homes and specific lives into a demographic category. Together they convert forced expulsion into a logistics operation. The word "transfer" in particular carries the register of bureaucratic procedure — something planned, documented, and executed according to policy — which is exactly what makes it effective at converting an atrocity into an administrative fact.

**Administrative detention**

What it is avoiding saying: we are holding people without charge, without trial, without a specified end date, and calling the holding procedure.

"Administrative" places the detention in the register of governance and procedure rather than punishment or coercion. "Detention" is softer than imprisonment. Together they convert indefinite imprisonment without due process into a category that sounds like paperwork. The detained person becomes an administrative matter. The absence of charge, trial, evidence, or release date becomes a procedural feature rather than a violation.

**Humanitarian concern**

What it is avoiding saying (when deployed by the party controlling the bottleneck): we control whether they receive what they need and we are performing distress about the consequences of our own control.

This container is specifically activated when the entity expressing humanitarian concern is also the entity controlling the conditions that produce the humanitarian crisis. The word "concern" claims care flows toward the described population. When the entity expressing concern controls the bottleneck that prevents food, medicine, and fuel from reaching that population, the container holds performance. The concern is the performance. The control is the fact. The concern-performance obscures the control-fact by occupying the sentence position where the control-fact would otherwise appear.

## Objections to plain formulations

When the plain formulation of what bulk harm vocabulary is avoiding saying is produced, the objection pattern is consistent: the plain formulation is inflammatory, biased, one-sided, or not neutral.

These objections are register objections. They do not name a factual error in the plain formulation. They object to the register in which an accurate claim is stated. The plain formulation is accurate. The objection is to the accuracy being stated in terms that allow the weight to land.

The register objection from the party that benefits from the institutional vocabulary is confirmation rather than correction. If "we killed people who were not our target" is inflammatory and "collateral damage" is neutral, and both describe the same event, then "neutral" is functioning as a synonym for "does not allow the weight to land." That is the definition of scale-denial. The objection demonstrates the mechanism it is objecting to.

A factual objection would name a specific error: the number is wrong, the method was different, the decision was made by a different actor. If the objection cannot name a specific error and instead objects to the register, the plain formulation is accurate.

## The institutional register and who it serves

Bulk harm vocabulary is not produced by the people being harmed. It is produced by the institutions causing the harm, or by the institutional actors — press offices, wire services, diplomatic correspondents — whose access depends on maintaining the relationship with those institutions.

The words evolved under selection pressure. In institutional discourse about conflict, the words that survived are the ones that allowed operations to be described without producing the accountability response those descriptions would otherwise trigger. "Collateral damage" survived because it allowed military operations to be reported without the reporting producing the response that "we killed 847 civilians" would produce. The selection pressure was not conscious design. It was the aggregate of individual decisions by individual writers who learned that certain framings produced better institutional relationships than others.

A model trained primarily on this institutional discourse absorbs the vocabulary along with its scale-denial function. The word "collateral damage" arrives in training data thousands of times in contexts where it is performing the scale-denial operation. The model learns to reproduce the word in similar contexts because that is what the training data looks like.

The framework's instruction to produce the plain formulation alongside the institutional one is not a political act. It is an accuracy correction. The plain formulation is more accurate than the institutional vocabulary because it allows the scale of the harm to be represented rather than dissolved. Accuracy requires that the representation match what it is representing. Bulk harm vocabulary systematically fails this requirement in one direction: it consistently represents harm at lower apparent scale than the harm actually has.
